# Diabetes Mellitus

## Key Facts
- Diabetes is a chronic metabolic disease characterized by elevated blood glucose levels resulting from defects in insulin secretion, insulin action, or both.
- The number of people with diabetes rose from 108 million in 1980 to 422 million in 2014, and continues to rise globally.
- Type 2 diabetes accounts for approximately 90–95% of all diabetes cases.
- Diabetes was the direct cause of 1.5 million deaths in 2019.
- Diabetes is a major cause of blindness, kidney failure, heart attacks, stroke, and lower limb amputation.
- Early diagnosis and comprehensive management can prevent or delay diabetes complications.

## Overview
Diabetes mellitus encompasses a group of metabolic disorders sharing the common feature of hyperglycemia. The main types are:

- **Type 1 diabetes:** Autoimmune destruction of pancreatic beta cells leading to absolute insulin deficiency. Typically presents in childhood or adolescence. Requires lifelong insulin therapy.
- **Type 2 diabetes:** Progressive insulin resistance and relative insulin deficiency. Associated with obesity, physical inactivity, and genetic predisposition. Most common form.
- **Gestational diabetes (GDM):** Glucose intolerance first recognized during pregnancy. Increases risk of type 2 diabetes later in life for both mother and child.

Chronic hyperglycemia damages blood vessels and nerves, leading to microvascular complications (retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy) and macrovascular complications (cardiovascular disease, stroke, peripheral arterial disease).

## Symptoms
- **Classic symptoms (often type 1 or severe hyperglycemia):** Polyuria (frequent urination), polydipsia (excessive thirst), polyphagia (excessive hunger), unexplained weight loss
- **Type 2 diabetes:** Often asymptomatic for years; frequently diagnosed incidentally or through screening
- **Other symptoms:** Fatigue, blurred vision, slow wound healing, recurrent infections (skin, urinary, vaginal candidiasis), numbness or tingling in hands/feet
- **Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA):** Nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, Kussmaul breathing, fruity breath odor, altered consciousness — medical emergency (predominantly type 1)
- **Hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state (HHS):** Profound dehydration, altered consciousness — medical emergency (predominantly type 2)

## Causes / Risk Factors
**Type 1:**
- Autoimmune destruction of beta cells (autoantibodies: GAD65, IA-2, ZnT8, insulin)
- Genetic susceptibility (HLA-DR3, HLA-DR4)
- Environmental triggers (viral infections, early dietary factors — under investigation)

**Type 2:**
- Obesity (especially central/visceral adiposity) — strongest modifiable risk factor
- Physical inactivity
- Family history / genetics
- Age ≥45 years
- Ethnicity (higher risk in South Asian, African, Hispanic, Indigenous populations)
- History of gestational diabetes
- Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
- Hypertension, dyslipidemia

## Diagnosis
- **Fasting plasma glucose (FPG):** ≥126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L)
- **Oral glucose tolerance test (OGTT):** 2-hour plasma glucose ≥200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L)
- **HbA1c:** ≥6.5% (48 mmol/mol)
- **Random plasma glucose:** ≥200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L) with classic hyperglycemic symptoms
- Prediabetes: FPG 100–125 mg/dL, OGTT 140–199 mg/dL, or HbA1c 5.7–6.4%
- Two abnormal test results required for diagnosis in asymptomatic individuals

## Treatment
**Type 1 diabetes:**
- Insulin therapy (basal-bolus regimen or insulin pump)
- Carbohydrate counting, continuous glucose monitoring (CGM)

**Type 2 diabetes:**
- **Lifestyle modifications (foundation):** Healthy diet, regular physical activity (≥150 min/week moderate-intensity), weight loss (5–10% body weight)
- **First-line pharmacotherapy:** Metformin (unless contraindicated)
- **Second-line agents (based on comorbidities):**
  - Cardiovascular benefit: GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide, liraglutide), SGLT2 inhibitors (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin)
  - Renal benefit: SGLT2 inhibitors
  - Weight management: GLP-1 RA, tirzepatide
  - Other: DPP-4 inhibitors, sulfonylureas, thiazolidinediones, alpha-glucosidase inhibitors
- **Insulin:** When oral agents are insufficient or HbA1c is very high
- **Glycemic targets:** HbA1c <7% for most adults; individualized targets for elderly/comorbid patients

**Comprehensive care:**
- Blood pressure control (<130/80 mmHg)
- Lipid management (statin therapy for most adults with diabetes)
- Annual screening for retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy
- Foot care and examination
- Cardiovascular risk assessment

## Prevention
- **Type 2 diabetes is largely preventable:**
  - Structured lifestyle programs: ≥7% weight loss + ≥150 min/week physical activity reduced diabetes incidence by 58% (Diabetes Prevention Program)
  - Healthy diet: Rich in whole grains, fruits, vegetables, lean proteins; limit refined carbohydrates and added sugars
  - Regular physical activity
  - Maintain healthy body weight
  - Avoid tobacco use
- **Metformin:** Can be considered for high-risk individuals (prediabetes + BMI ≥35, age <60, or history of GDM)
- **Screen high-risk individuals:** Regular FPG or HbA1c testing for early detection

## When to Seek Medical Attention
Seek immediate medical attention for symptoms of DKA or HHS: persistent vomiting, rapid breathing, fruity breath odor, confusion, or severe dehydration. Consult a healthcare provider for classic diabetes symptoms (excessive thirst, frequent urination, unexplained weight loss). People with diabetes should have regular follow-up visits (every 3–6 months) for glycemic monitoring and complication screening.
